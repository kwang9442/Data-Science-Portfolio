About Me

I'm a data science and sociology major working in sports performance analytics and machine learning. I currently work with the University of Oregon D1 Sports Science Program, focusing on volleyball and football athlete data to help coaches and staff make more informed decisions about workload, readiness, and recovery.

This site showcases a collection of projects demonstrating the tools and methods I use, from clustering and signal processing to play detection and movement analysis.

D1 Volleyball CMJ Load & Readiness Modeling

This project analyzes NCAA Division I volleyball force-plate data to evaluate how short-term and medium-term workload impacts athlete readiness and recovery. More specifically, countermovement jump metrics such as RSI_Modified and Jump Height are evaluated using mixed-effects models, load feature engineering, and athlete-specific visualizations.

Methods

Mixed-effects modeling, feature engineering, regression analysis, and regression visualization

Impact

Helped evaluate relationships between training load and recovery trends for coaching staff.

D1 Football Play Detection

An analytics pipeline built for NCAA D1 football tracking data that identifies individual plays using velocity-based segmentation, change detection, practice-period matching, and trajectory clustering. The pipeline automates athlete movement interpretation at scale and provides a reproducible structure for segmenting raw tracking streams into meaningful plays.

Confidentiality Notice: Due to our schools' data policies, I cannot display raw tracking datasets, full movement distributions, or athlete-identifying information. All examples shown use derived, anonymized, or illustrative subsets.

Methods Used

  • Velocity peak-based segmentation to define play boundaries
  • Abrupt movement change detection to refine transitions
  • Interval Tree matching to map plays to practice periods
  • K-Means clustering of player movement trajectories
