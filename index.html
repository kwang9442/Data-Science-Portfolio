D1 Volleyball CMJ Load & Readiness Modeling

A full mixed-effects modeling study examining how short- and medium-term workload influences a players' readiness in NCAA Division I volleyball athletes. Using force-plate countermovement jump (CMJ) metrics collected before and after practices and games, this project evaluates whether 7-day and 21-day load metrics meaningfully predict RSI_Modified, jump height, and overall performance readiness.

The analysis incorporates random-effects modeling to account for differences between athletes, separating within-athlete daily fluctuations from between-athlete load profiles. This project analyzes Division I collegiate volleyball Countermovement Jump (CMJ) data to understand how short-term and medium-term training loads influence athlete readiness. CMJ metrics such as RSI_Modified and Jump Height are evaluated using mixed-effects models, load feature engineering, and athlete-specific visualizations.

Confidentiality Notice: Due to our schools' data policies, raw force-plate outputs and athlete-identifying metrics cannot be displayed. All visualizations and code shared here use de-identified or derived summaries.

Methods Used

  • Mixed-effects modeling with random effects intercepts for each athlete
  • Within- vs between-athlete load decomposition
  • Load-ratio feature engineering (7-day vs 21-day spikes)
  • Pre/post-practice workload adjustment
  • Regression visualization of individualized load-response relationships

D1 Football Play Detection

An analytics pipeline built for NCAA D1 football tracking data that identifies individual plays using velocity-based segmentation, change detection, practice-period matching, and trajectory clustering. The pipeline automates athlete movement interpretation at scale and provides a reproducible structure for segmenting raw tracking streams into meaningful plays.

Confidentiality Notice: Due to our schools' data policies, I cannot display raw tracking datasets, full movement distributions, or athlete-identifying information. All examples shown use derived, anonymized, or illustrative subsets.

Methods Used

  • Velocity peak-based segmentation to define play boundaries
  • Abrupt movement change detection to refine transitions
  • Interval Tree matching to map plays to practice periods
  • K-Means clustering of player movement trajectories
