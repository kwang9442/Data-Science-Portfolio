About Me

I'm a Data Scientist specializing in sports performance analytics, machine learning, and player-tracking data. I currently work with the University of Oregon D1 Sports Science Program, focusing on volleyball and football athlete data to help coaches make informed decisions about workload, readiness, and recovery.

This site showcases a collection of projects demonstrating the tools and methods I use, from clustering and signal processing to play detection and movement analysis.

Featured Project: D1 Football Play Detection

A full analytics pipeline built for NCAA D1 football tracking data that identifies individual plays using velocity-based segmentation, abrupt-change detection, practice-period matching, and trajectory clustering.

Confidentiality Notice: Due to NCAA Division 1 data security policies, I cannot display raw tracking datasets, full movement distributions, or athlete-identifying information. All examples shown on this website use derived, anonymized, or illustrative subsets.

Methods Used

  • Velocity peak-based segmentation to define play boundaries
  • Abrupt movement change detection to refine transitions
  • Interval Tree matching to link plays to specific practice periods
  • K-Means clustering of player movement trajectories
